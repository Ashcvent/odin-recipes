Mangu

Description

Mangú is a traditional Dominican dish made from boiled green plantains that are mashed to a smooth, slightly chunky consistency.
Typically, the plantains are softened by boiling, then mashed with butter, olive oil, or the water they were boiled in, resulting in a creamy yet hearty texture.
Mangú is usually served as part of a classic Dominican breakfast called Los Tres Golpes, which includes fried eggs, fried cheese, and salami, often accompanied by pickled red onions for a tangy contrast.

Ingredients

  • Plantains
  • Salt
  • Butter
  • Milk

Steps

  • Peel the plantains
  • Add water, salt, and the plaintains to a pot
  • Boil the plaintains until they are tender
  • Drain the water
  • (As needed) Gradually add butter and milk to the pot of plantains
  • Mash the plantains
  • Serve with sides
